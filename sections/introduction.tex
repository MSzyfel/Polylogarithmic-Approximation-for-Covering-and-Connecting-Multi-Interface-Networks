\section{Introduction}
Designing Internet of Things (IoT) networks often demands establishing connection between large number of devices, which can be achieved by choosing between large variety of interfaces available at each device. Moreover, the devices present in the network may vary according to the interfaces they provide for the usage. For example some device may be able to connect by Bluetooth and Wi-Fi, while the others may provide Zigbee and Z-Wave interfaces. Since IoT devices are typically resource-constrained, it may be desirable to try to establish every relevant connection, while minimizing the amount of resources required to do so.

We model the above problem as one of two graph problems, depending on the type of the connection one wishes to establish. Each devices in the network is modeled as vertex of a graph. Additionally, with each such vertex we associate a set of labels representing the available interfaces. In the covering problem each edge in this graph represents a connection that we wish to establish by assigning a common interface to its endpoints. 
To see an example, see Figure~\ref{example_covering}. In the connection problem we are additionally given a set of terminals $U\subseteq V$, each edge can be activated by assigning a common interface to its endpoints and we require that the subgraph of activated edges connects all terminals (vertices outside $U$ may be used as Steiner vertices). To see an example, see Figure~\ref{example_connecting}. In both problems, we assume that activating an interface at a given vertex incurs some arbitrary cost. The goal is to minimize either the min-max cost (the maximum cost per vertex) or min-sum cost (sum of costs over all vertices) of the interface assignment. Hereby, we focus ourselved on the min-max cost criterion.

\subsection{Related Work}
The algorithmic study of multi-interface networks goes back at least to the work of Klasing, Kosowski, and Navarra \cite{CostMinimizationInWirelessNetworks}. When $k$ is bounded they show APX-hardness and provide an $\min\brc{\cl{\frac{k+1}{2}, \frac{2m}{n}}}$-approximation algorithm for the min-sum version of the covering problem with uniform costs. For unbounded $k$ they give an $\min\brc{k-1, \sqrt{n}\br{1+\ln n}}$-approximation for interface-dependent costs and prove that the problem is NP-hard to approximate within $O\br{\log k}$-factor.
Assuming $U=V$, the min-sum connection problem for $k\geq 2$ is known to be APX-hard even for uniform costs and a $\br{2-1/k}$-approximation algorithm is known as well as $4/3$-approximation for $k=2$. Non uniform, interface dependent costs version admits $2$-approximation \cite{ExploitingMultiInterfaceNetworksConnectivityAndCheapestPaths}. The same research direction also led to results for other classical optimization problems in the multi-interface setting, including maximum matching \cite{MaximumMatchingInMultiInterfaceNetworks} and flow optimization with bandwidth constraints \cite{FlowProblemsInMultiInterfaceNetworks}.

The min-max coverage problem is known to be NP-hard for any fixed $\Delta\geq 5$ and $k\geq 16$ and cannot be approximated within a factor of $\eta\ln\Delta$ unless P=NP even for uniform costs on trees (for a certain constant $\eta$). On the algorithmic side the problem admits an $O\br{\ln\Delta+b\cdot\min\brc{\ln \Delta,c_{\text{max}}}}$-approximation algorithm, where $b$ denotes the existence of a $b$-bounded ownership function as well as $O\brc{k\cdot c_{max}}$ and $O\brc{\Delta\cdot c_{max}}$-approximation algorithms.
Assuming $U=V$, the min-max connection is known to be NP-hard for any fixed $\Delta\geq 3$ and $k\geq 10$, but is solvable for $k\leq 2$. By equivalence, for the case of trees the problem admits $O\br{\log n}$-approximation (since for trees $b=1$) and $O\brc{k\cdot c_{max}}$ and $O\brc{\Delta\cdot c_{max}}$-approximation. No such results are known for the general case \cite{MinimizeTheMaximumDutyInMultiInterfaceNetworks}

Another active line of research concerns constrained and parameterized versions of coverage. In the $p$-\emph{coverage} setting, where each node may activate at most $p$ interfaces, the problem is NP-complete already for $p=2$ on graphs with maximum degree $\Delta \geq 4$, even with uniform costs~\cite{ConstrainedConnectivityInBoundedXWidthMultiInterfaceNetworks}. Aloisio and Navarra showed that for $p=2$ the problem is FPT with respect to treewidth $\text{tw}$ via Courcelle's theorem. They also gave an explicit dynamic programming algorithms, for carvingwidth and pathwidth \cite{ConstrainedConnectivityInBoundedXWidthMultiInterfaceNetworks}. The pathwidth case was subsequently revisited and extended in~\cite{OnCoverageInMultiInterfaceNetworksWithBoundedPathwidth}. 
% Budget-constrained variants, where the activation cost at each node must not exceed a fixed budget, were analyzed from the viewpoint of treewidth and branchwidth in~\cite{OnBudgetConstrainedCoverageInMultiInterfaceNetworks}, again yielding polynomial-time algorithms for bounded-parameter graph classes. 
Most recently the covering problem was studied in \cite{OnComputationalComplexityOfCoveringMultiInterfaceNetworks}, establishing FPT results including an algorithm parametrized by $k+\text{tw}$ and hardness lower bounds with respect to several structural parameters, including vertex cover number, feedback edge number and distance to clique.

\subsection{Our contribution and techniques}
In this paper we give a first $O\br{\log m}$-approximation algorithm for the min-max connection problem with non-uniform costs. This improves the previously known result of \cite{MinimizeTheMaximumDutyInMultiInterfaceNetworks}, which gave an $O\br{\ln\Delta+b\cdot\min\brc{\ln \Delta,c_{\text{max}}}}$-approximation for the min-max covering version, where $b$ is the parameter related to the existence of a $b$-owner function \cite{SemidynamicAlgorithmsForMaintainingSingleSourceShortestPathTrees,PlanarOrientationsWithLowOutDegreeAndCompactionOfAdjacencyMatrices}. This als improves the $O\br{\sqrt{n}\cdot\log n}$-approximation for the min-sum covering problem by \cite{CostMinimizationInWirelessNetworks}. Our approach is based on a set-cover-style LP relaxation together with a randomized rounding. We also show how to use the same LP relaxation to obtain a $k$-approximation algorithm for the min-sum and min-max covering problems, where $k$ is the number of interfaces. All these guarantees hold in the non-uniform setting where activation costs depend on both the interface and the vertex it is assigned to. 

% Then, we shift to combinatorial algorithms. We give a $\Delta$-approximation algorithm for min-sum covering, a $1.39\Delta$-approximation algorithm for min-sum connection, and a $2\Delta$-approximation algorithm for min-max covering, where $\Delta$ is the maximum degree of the input graph. In particular, for min-max covering this improves on the previously known $O\br{\Delta\cdot c_{\text{max}}}$ approximation, where $c_{\text{max}}$ is the maximum interface-activation cost.

% We complement these positive results with hardness: we show that both the min-sum covering and connecting problems cannot be approximated within a factor of $\br{1-o\br{1}}\cdot\ln n$ even on stars, unless $P=NP$. We also prove that even the unit-cost min-sum covering assignment problem on bipartite graphs cannot be approximated within a factor of $o\br{\log \Delta}$ unless $P=NP$. Since analogous logarithmic hardness is known for min-max variants of the problems \cite{MinimizeTheMaximumDutyInMultiInterfaceNetworks}, our $O\br{\log m}$-approximation guarantees are tight up to constant factors for the case when $\Delta$ is unbounded.
